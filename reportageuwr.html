A member of UNSW UWR swims across to the middle of the pool before diving to collect the ball sitting on the floor during a training session. Two teams compete for the initial control of the ball before goals are scored, which gives control to the defending team.
Game play is fast and occurs underwater. Players are allowed to break the surface for air during play making timing important.
Opposing teams are discerned by the color of their headgear. Defending teams are allowed to tackle and intercept an attacker with the ball.
UNSW UWR members practice a pass. The weighted ball used in UWR is filled with salt water allowing it to sink in the pool.
A defender (left of center, bottom) watches the attacking team as he lies on top of his team's goal. Attackers are allowed to pull defenders out of the way as they try for a goal. Defending teams are allowed to swap out "goalies" as they come up for air.
A player swims across the pitch whilst being supported by team mates below.
A member secures the ball as he makes a break after taking the ball from an opposition.
A member dives after a loose ball resulting from a bad pass.
UNSW UWR aspire to have Ausstralia represented at the Underwater Rugby World Championships. The next scheduled championships are to be held in Columbia, 2015.

underwater rugby

The University of New South Wales Underwater Rugby Club (UNSW UWR) has been an instrumental organisation in the introduction of the sport to Australia in 2007.

The coed club consists of a dedicated core of individuals who organise training sessions, introduce the sport to new comers, raise awareness and train. Revolving around the group are a mixture of students, professionals and international visitors.
